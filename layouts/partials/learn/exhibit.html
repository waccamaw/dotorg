DIGITAL MUSEUM — PAST, PRESENT & FUTURE

Waccamaw Indian People: Past, Present & Future is a digital museum and living archive, built in partnership with the Waccamaw Indian People, Coastal Carolina University, the Horry County Museum, and community volunteers. It honors the people whose traditional homelands include the ground where the university now stands — and it insists, in its own words, that "Native American people are not just a 'people of the past.' They are our friends, neighbors and community members."

The exhibit unfolds in three movements — Past, Present, and Future — and includes:

  • Waccamaw Voices — oral histories told in the words of tribal members and elders.
  • The Ecological & Cultural Interpretive Trail — a walking trail at 591 Bluewater Road, Aynor, SC, where the land itself carries the story.
  • A journey from the Dimery Settlement through state recognition in 2005 and on toward the tribe's ongoing pursuit of federal acknowledgement.
