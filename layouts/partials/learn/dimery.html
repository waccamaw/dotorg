THE DIMERY SETTLEMENT

Long before the tribe formally organized in 1992, the Waccamaw endured as a community. Near Dog Bluff, in what is now Horry County, families lived, farmed, and raised their children together in the settlement that came to carry the Dimery name — a close-knit people who held onto their identity across generations when claiming it could be dangerous.

The settlement had its own gathering places and its own schoolhouse, the Pine Level (Dimery) School, where Waccamaw children learned together in a community the wider world often refused to see. Today's tribal members trace their lineage through the many descendants of the Dimery Settlement — it is the taproot from which the modern tribe grew, and the web of family lines the tribe's genealogist still keeps today. More than three hundred years of continuous presence run through this one stretch of Carolina ground.
