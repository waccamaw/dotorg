Who We're Writing For#

Waccamaw.org is, first and foremost, a public and educational site — a place for people who are not tribal members to learn who the Waccamaw are, and for members to find services. When in doubt, write for these two readers.

🎒

Students & Educators (K–12)

Teachers, students, and young learners meeting the Waccamaw for the first time — often from a class assignment, a field trip, or the Pauwau.

They need

  • Plain, accurate language and short paragraphs
  • Terms defined — Pauwau, sovereignty, recognition
  • The "why it matters" made explicit
  • Nothing gated or requiring an account

Write & design: present-tense, living-culture framing ("we are," not "we were"); scannable headings; captioned images; factual and age-appropriate.

🌳

Roots & Genealogy Seekers

Adults — often older — researching family history, tracing a line back to the Dimery Settlement, or wondering whether they descend from the Waccamaw.

They need

  • Depth, provenance, real dates, places, and records
  • Clear pathways to the tribal roll, genealogy, and membership
  • Named people and places they can search
  • Comfortable, larger, readable text

Write & design: respectful and substantive; cite sources; connect the history to living families; point to the Genealogist, the Member Portal, and Contact.

About the Red Hawk Symbol

The red hawk holds deep significance in our tribal culture, representing:

  • Vision — Seeing the larger picture and planning ahead
  • Awareness — Being attuned to our surroundings and community
  • Connection — Bridging the spiritual and natural worlds
  • Protection — Watching over our people and our land

This imagery connects us to our ancestors and the Dog Bluff community we've called home for over 300 years.

Circular Text (Circumscription)#

The current logo features the text "The People of The Waccamaw" in a circular band around the central hawk and dreamcatcher design. This design element warrants reconsideration.

Current circular text reads:
"The People of The Waccamaw"

Linguistic Consideration

While the exact etymology of "Waccamaw" is debated among linguists, several sources suggest it may derive from Siouan language roots potentially related to the concept of "people" or a similar communal meaning.

Note: Definitive etymological sources for "Waccamaw" are limited. The South Carolina Department of Archives and History and tribal oral histories provide the most authoritative accounts of our name's origins.

Call for More Meaningful Text

If the tribal name already carries communal meaning, the phrase "The People of The..." may be redundant. Additionally, nearly half the words ("The," "of," "The") are articles and prepositions that add little cultural significance.

Design terminology: This circular text element is called a circumscription in logo design, or alternatively circular text or ring text.

We encourage tribal members and designers to propose more culturally meaningful text that:

  • Honors our Siouan linguistic heritage
  • Reflects our cultural values and identity
  • Reduces redundancy and filler words
  • Works well in circular composition

💡 Ideas for consideration: Phrases in our ancestral language, our founding year (or state recognition year), cultural values like "Vision • Protection • Community," or geographic markers like "Dog Bluff, South Carolina" could all serve as more meaningful alternatives.

Usage Guidelines#

What's Published

  • Public meetings only — Only meetings marked as "public" are included in the podcast feed
  • Full recordings — Complete audio from start to finish, no editing
  • Regular schedule — New episodes added as public meetings occur (typically monthly)

How to Subscribe

  1. Choose a podcast app — Apple Podcasts, Overcast, Spotify, or any app that supports RSS feeds
  2. Search for "Waccamaw Public Meetings" — or use the platform buttons above
  3. Subscribe — New episodes will automatically download when available
  4. Alternative: Add RSS feed manually — Copy the RSS feed URL and paste it into your podcast app's "Add by URL" feature

Usage Guidelines#

Accessibility#

  • All colors meet WCAG AA contrast standards for text
  • Interactive elements have clear focus states
  • Images include alt text
  • Semantic HTML structure throughout

Responsive Design#

  • Mobile-first approach with breakpoint at 768px
  • Flexible layouts using CSS Grid and Flexbox
  • Logo and navigation adapt for smaller screens
