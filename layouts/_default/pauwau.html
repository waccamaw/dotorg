About the gathering

Where our heritage lives out loud

Each year on the first full weekend of November, the Waccamaw Indian People open our tribal grounds for the Pauwau & Cultural Arts Festival — a gathering of traditional dancing, drumming, storytelling, and fellowship in the heart of the South Carolina Lowcountry.

A Pauwau (also spelled “powwow”) is a vibrant celebration of Native American culture. Ours honors the heritage of the Waccamaw people while welcoming intertribal connections and all visitors who wish to learn about and respect indigenous traditions. In more than three decades, we have missed only two years — during the COVID-19 pandemic — a testament to our commitment to preserving and sharing who we are.

Our land

The Waccamaw Tribal Grounds

Our home sits on Bluewater Road in Aynor, in the Dog Bluff community of Horry County — land with deep and continuous roots for the Waccamaw people.

In 2004 the Waccamaw Indian People of S.C. purchased this site for their tribal headquarters, becoming a state-recognized Indian tribe in 2005. The tribe traces historical ties to the Dimery Settlement, a community of Native families founded in the early 1800s in Dog Bluff Township. — South Carolina Historical Marker, Waccamaw Tribal Grounds

Today the grounds are headquarters, gathering place, and ceremonial home — and each November they become the stage for our Pauwau.
