About School Day

Heritage, taught hands-on

Each year, the Thursday and Friday before our Pauwau & Cultural Arts Festival are dedicated to School Day — a two-day educational program welcoming students, teachers, parents, and homeschooled children to experience Waccamaw culture, traditions, and history in an immersive learning environment on the Tribal Grounds.

School Day offers hands-on learning aligned with South Carolina curriculum standards, bringing Native American history and culture to life for students of all ages.

Who it’s for

Built for classrooms and families

Teachers & Schools

Bring a class or an entire school for a day of cultural education:

  • Traditional dance demonstrations
  • Indigenous arts and crafts workshops
  • Storytelling and oral history
  • Materials aligned with SC standards
  • Q&A with tribal members and educators
  • Cultural preservation and language programs

Parents & Guardians

Parents and guardians are welcome to attend alongside their children — a chance to:

  • Learn alongside your students
  • Take part in hands-on cultural activities
  • Meet tribal members and educators
  • Support indigenous cultural preservation
  • Create meaningful family memories
  • Deepen understanding of local Native heritage

Students

Students engage in interactive, age-appropriate learning:

  • Hands-on traditional crafts and art
  • Indigenous games and activities
  • Cultural demonstrations and performances
  • History of the Waccamaw people
  • Activities for elementary through high school
  • Chances to ask questions and engage directly
On the grounds

Learning rooted in place

School Day takes place on the Waccamaw Tribal Grounds, where interpretive exhibits, the dance arena, and tribal members themselves turn the day into a living classroom — history told by the people who carry it.

Details & logistics

Planning your visit

Date & time

Thursday & Friday before Pauwau weekend (ahead of {{ $when }}). Times announced with registration; two full days.

Location

Waccamaw Tribal Grounds

Transportation & parking

School buses and parent vehicles welcome. Ample parking on the grounds; bus drop-off and pick-up areas clearly marked.

Accessibility

The grounds are accessible. Please note any accommodations needed on your registration form.

Lunch & refreshments

Schools should plan for lunch. Water and light refreshments may be provided; details come with your confirmation.

What to wear

Comfortable clothing and walking shoes. Activities may be outdoors, so dress for the weather.

Chaperones

Schools must meet teacher and chaperone ratios per district policy. All attending adults must be registered.

Admission

Admission details are provided during registration. Financial assistance may be available for qualifying schools.

Curriculum alignment

Mapped to SC standards

School Day programming supports South Carolina social studies and cultural education standards:

Elementary

  • Native American cultures and traditions
  • South Carolina history and indigenous peoples
  • Cultural diversity and respect
  • Art, music, and storytelling traditions

Middle & high school

  • Indigenous history and sovereignty
  • Cultural preservation and modern tribal life
  • Anthropology and archaeology
  • Social justice and indigenous rights
Questions

Frequently asked

How do I register my school or class?

Registration opens in March {{ $year }} through our online portal. Teachers and administrators can register classes, grade levels, or whole schools.

Can homeschool families attend?

Yes — homeschool families and co-ops are welcome. Register through the School Day portal and note your homeschool group information.

What if my school can’t attend School Day?

The full Pauwau & Cultural Arts Festival weekend is open to all visitors. See the main Pauwau page for weekend details.

Are there age restrictions?

School Day suits all K–12 grade levels, with programming designed to engage elementary through high school students.

Questions about School Day?

For questions about programming or registration, reach the Tribal Office — select “Pauwau” as your inquiry category.
