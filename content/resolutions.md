**Breakdown of Constitution:**

| Category | Location | Description |
| :---- | :---- | :---- |
| Jurisdiction | Article II | Jurisdiction extends to all lands/property now held by the tribe and to anyone who lives on or comes onto those lands/property. |
| Mission | Article III | Respect, protect, and promote our rights to self-determination and self-sufficiency; to encompass our spiritual and cultural traditions; to create, maintain and provide health, social, employment and educational opportunities and services; to preserve and conserve the resources of Mother Earth for the advancement and prosperity for our present and seven generations yet unborn.   |
| Membership/Association | Article IV | 66% of all members must be of lineal descent of Indian members of the “Dimery Settlement.” Membership is afforded to those who meet this, and who are deemed appropriate by majority Council vote. |
| Full Membership | Article IV, Section 1 | Full members may vote in tribal elections, hold office, share in any distribution of tribal assets and to share assistance afforded to tribal members. |
| Full Membership | Article IV, Section 2 | Full members may serve in/on committees at the discretion of Council.  |
| Full Membership | Article IV, Section 3 | Makes membership an “internal asset” and retain membership at leisure of Council.  |
| Full Membership | Article IV, Section 4 | Members must pay fees, as determined by Council.  |
| Membership | Article IV, Section 5 | Members may not hold dual tribal membership. |
| Honorary Membership | Article IV, Section B | Honorary membership may be given to any person determined to be worthy by the Tribal Chief. It is a temporary status- 7 years- and can be renewed or terminated. |
| Honorary Membership | Article IV, Section B, Subsection 1 | Not allowed to vote in tribal elections, hold tribal government positions, or benefit from assets or assistance. |
| Honorary Membership | Article IV, Section B, Subsection 2 | Allowed to serve on committees in any capacity at discretion of Council. |
| Honorary Membership | Article IV, Section B, Subsection 3 | May be awarded full membership due to contribution and based on recommendation of Chief and majority vote of Council. |
| Honorary Membership | Article IV, Section B, Subsection 4 | Subject to ID card renewal fees as laid out by Council. |
| Honorary Membership | Article IV, Section B, Subsection 5 | Membership will not be revoked without cause.  |
| Honorary Membership | Article IV, Section B, Subsection 6 | No access/adjudication by Tribal Elders or Judges |
| Governing Body | Article V | Establishes 3 separate branches of Tribal government. Establishes 30 days to challenge any votes, mandates, motions and proposals. |
| Governing Body | Article V, Section A | Establishes the Executive Branch as the Tribal Chief, Vice Chief, and two second Chiefs. |
| Governing Body | Article V, Section A, Subsection 1 | Tribal Chief shall be a full member with genealogical ties to the settlement, and who will be elected by a vote of all full tribal members. The vote will take place during elections for even numbered Tribal Council seat elections. |
| Governing Body | Article V, Section A, Subsection 2 | Tribal Chief serves for 4 years and until a successor is chosen. 2nd Chiefs leave office when the Chief is voted out or retires, unless otherwise removed.  |
| Governing Body | Article V, Section A, Subsection 3 | Tribal Chief controls all matters external, but subject to oversight by the Tribal Council. Should the Tribal Council challenge such a decision, it shall be binding upon the tribe unless it is overturned by an affirmative vote of at least six of the seven council members called to a public meeting and required to vote specifically on overturning that decision.  |
| Governing Body | Article V, Section A, Subsection 4 | Chief maintains “Veto” power over Council decisions. Council can overturn the veto with a 5 of 7 person vote. |
| Governing Body | Article V, Section A, Subsection 5 | Vice Chief must be a member with genealogical ties to the settlement and is elected along with Tribal Chief. |
| Governing Body | Article V, Section A, Subsection 6 | Vice and Second Chiefs can call for revotes on any Council votes.  |
| Governing Body | Article V, Section A, Subsection 7 | Tribal Chief makes sure external affairs are considered in internal affairs, but the Council has authority.  |
| Governing Body | Article V, Section B | Establishes the Legislative Branch as consisting of 7 elected representatives making up a Tribal Council and Chief of Tribal Council. |
| Governing Body | Article V, Section B, Subsection 1 | Tribal Council members are elected for 4 years, and until the next person is elected and seated. Odd number seats are elected in the year of Presidential elections. Even number seats are elected along with the Tribal Chief are two years later. |
| Governing Body | Article V, Section B, Subsection 2 | All internal matters are in the control of the Council. However, the Chief can veto those decisions. |
| Governing Body | Article V, Section B, Subsection 3 | Chief of Council is elected by voting members of the tribe, and will coincide with odd numbered seat elections for Council.  |
| Governing Body | Article V, Section B, Subsection 4 | Chief of Council controls open meetings, sets agendas, calls for online votes, monitors proxy votes, and controls tribal meetings. They serve for four years.  |
| Governing Body | Article V, Section B, Subsection 5 | Members of Council are allotted time to express views concerning issues prior to being presented to Council for a decision. |
| Governing Body | Article V, Section B, Subsection 6 | Chief of Council and the Council has one vote on issues.  Members who are absent may designate a Second Chief to vote as a proxy. They vote in the way designated by the member, and are counted as a voting member. |
| Governing Body | Article V, Section B, Subsection 7 | No actions will be taken, that impact general membership of the tribe, without 3 readings before the tribal community. This can be excepted with permission from the Chief and Tribal Council majority vote. |
| Governing Body | Article V, Section B, Subsection 8 | Executive sessions, closed to the general membership may be called for and granted with permission of the Chief and majority of the Council. No votes impacting the general membership will be held in anything other than an open meeting.  |
| Governing Body | Article V, Section B, Subsection 9 | Quorum is met when four of seven Council members are present. In the case of only 4 in attendance, 3 votes counts for a decision. A tie results in waiting for the next meeting to make a decision. |
| Governing Body | Article V, Section B, Subsection 10 | Ordinances, proposals, resolutions, and other issues which receive a majority of the votes taken will be declared passed unless otherwise indicated. |
| Governing Body | Article V, Section B, Subsection 11 | Tribal Administrator can call for a revote on any issue before Council.  |
