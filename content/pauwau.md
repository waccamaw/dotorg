# Annual Pauwau Celebration

The Waccamaw Indian People host our annual Pauwau during the **first week of November** each year, celebrating our living culture and welcoming visitors to experience authentic indigenous traditions in the heart of South Carolina's Lowcountry.

## What is a Pauwau?

A Pauwau (also commonly spelled "powwow") is a vibrant gathering that celebrates Native American culture through traditional dancing, drumming, storytelling, and community fellowship. Our annual event showcases the rich heritage of the Waccamaw people while honoring intertribal connections and welcoming all who wish to learn about and respect indigenous traditions.

## Our Celebration

The Waccamaw Pauwau brings together:

- **Traditional Dancing** - Witness authentic indigenous dance styles passed down through generations
- **Drum Circles** - Experience the heartbeat of our culture through powerful drumming traditions
- **Craft Vendors** - Browse and purchase handmade indigenous arts, crafts, and traditional items
- **Traditional Foods** - Sample authentic Native American cuisine and regional specialties
- **Cultural Education** - Learn about Waccamaw history, traditions, and contemporary tribal life
- **Community Gathering** - Connect with tribal members, indigenous families, and visitors from across the region

## Visitor Information

All are welcome to attend the Waccamaw Pauwau and experience our cultural traditions. We ask that visitors:

- **Show Respect** - Photography may be restricted during certain sacred ceremonies
- **Support Indigenous Vendors** - Purchase directly from Native American artisans and craftspeople
- **Engage Respectfully** - Ask questions, learn about our culture, and participate with an open heart
- **Follow Guidance** - Listen to announcements and respect ceremonial protocols
